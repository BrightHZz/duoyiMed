# Results

## Study Population

The analytical sample comprised 7,810 participants (mean age 68.6 ± 7.2 years, 50% female, 36% urban). Mean education was 5.9 years. Median per-capita monthly income was 133 yuan. The SES composite score ranged from −0.79 to +2.94 (mean 0.00, standardized).

## Overall SES Effect on Biological Aging

The overall ATE of low SES on biological age was −0.118 (Table 1), with low-SES participants showing slightly lower (better) biological age scores. This overall effect masked substantial heterogeneity across subgroups, including a direction reversal in rural residents.

## Heterogeneous Treatment Effects

The association between SES and biological aging varied considerably across population subgroups (Table 1). The largest CATE magnitudes were observed in low-CES-D participants (CATE = −0.137) and those with low grip strength (CATE = −0.132). Notably, the direction reversed in rural residents (CATE = +0.071), where low SES was associated with higher biological age—the only subgroup showing the expected SES-health gradient direction. The association was attenuated in high-grip participants (CATE = −0.028) and urban residents (CATE = −0.044).

## SES × Physical Function Interaction

Cross-classification of SES and grip strength revealed a 0.73 SD gradient in biological aging, driven primarily by grip strength (Table 2). Both high-grip groups showed favorable biological age (−0.22 to −0.25), while both low-grip groups showed elevated biological age (+0.35 to +0.49). Low-SES participants with preserved grip strength had the lowest biological age (−0.25), even lower than high-SES high-grip participants (−0.22). Within low-grip strata, high-SES participants showed worse biological aging (+0.49) than low-SES participants (+0.35).

## Mediation Through Psychological Distress

In mediation analysis with the corrected 4-marker biological age composite (excluding CES-D to avoid circularity), the total effect of SES score on biological age was +0.019 per SD increase in SES (adjusted for age and sex). The indirect effect through CES-D was +0.012, and the direct effect (not through CES-D) was +0.007. CES-D mediated 63.0% of the total SES–biological age association, indicating that depressive symptoms are a substantial pathway linking SES to biological aging in this population.

## Sensitivity to Unmeasured Confounding

The E-value for the observed SES–biological aging association was 1.15, indicating that an unmeasured confounder would need to be associated with both SES and biological age by a risk ratio of at least 1.15 to fully explain the observed association, suggesting limited to moderate robustness to unmeasured confounding.
