We submit our manuscript entitled "Machine Learning-Based 7-Year Life Expectancy Prediction Integrating Geriatric Assessment in Older Chinese Men: A CHARLS Cohort Study" for consideration for publication in BMC Geriatrics.

Clinical guidelines from the USPSTF, AUA, EAU, and NCCN recommend that prostate cancer screening decisions in older men be guided by life expectancy rather than chronological age. However, existing life expectancy calculators (Schonberg index, Lee index) were developed in Western populations and do not incorporate direct measures of geriatric assessment domains—frailty, physical performance, and cognitive function—that are known independent predictors of mortality. We developed and internally validated machine learning models integrating comprehensive geriatric assessment to predict 7-year all-cause mortality in 2,398 community-dwelling Chinese men aged 60–75 years from the nationally representative China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS). Thirty-nine predictors spanning eight clinical domains were evaluated using logistic regression, random forest, XGBoost, and a stacking ensemble, with nested cross-validation.

The best-performing model (L1-regularized logistic regression) achieved an AUC-ROC of 0.683, falling short of the 0.80 threshold required for standalone clinical decision support. However, the model demonstrated a negative predictive value of 0.87, supporting its potential use as a rule-out tool to identify men who can safely continue PSA screening. The addition of comprehensive geriatric assessment domains did not yield clinically meaningful improvement over parsimonious models using only variables available in the Schonberg and Lee indices (delta AUC = 0.025; P = 0.108). Domain-aggregated feature importance identified disease burden (25.3%), functional status (17.9%), and frailty (11.7%) as the top contributors to prediction, while age alone accounted for only 4.6% of total importance—suggesting that geriatric vulnerability, rather than chronological age, drives mortality risk. We believe these findings, including the identification of an AUC ceiling of approximately 0.68 for all-cause mortality prediction in community-dwelling older adults, represent an important contribution to the clinical prediction literature and to the evidence base for geriatric-informed cancer screening decisions.

We believe BMC Geriatrics is the appropriate venue for this work because the journal prioritizes research at the intersection of geriatric assessment, clinical decision-making, and aging populations—precisely the focus of this study. The use of a nationally representative Chinese cohort addresses a geographic gap in the literature, as the vast majority of life expectancy prediction research has been conducted in Western populations.
